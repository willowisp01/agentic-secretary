# Parental Leave

## Eligibility

Available to any employee who has completed at least 6 months of
continuous service at the time of the expected delivery or adoption
placement date. Applies to the primary caregiver, regardless of gender,
and covers both birth and legally finalised adoption.

## Duration & Pay

Eligible employees receive 12 weeks of fully paid parental leave, taken
either continuously or split into two blocks within the first year after
birth or placement, subject to manager approval on timing.

## Notice & Approval Process

Submit a written request to your manager and HR at least 8 weeks before
the expected start date, except in cases of early delivery, where notice
should be given as soon as reasonably possible.

## Exceptions

Employees who have not yet reached 6 months of service may take unpaid
parental leave at management's discretion, capped at 4 weeks.

# Medical Leave

## Eligibility

Available to any employee who has completed at least 90 days of
continuous service. Covers personal illness or injury that prevents an
employee from performing their normal duties, as certified by a
registered medical practitioner.

## Duration & Pay

Eligible employees receive up to 6 weeks of fully paid medical leave per
calendar year. A medical certificate is required for any absence longer
than 2 consecutive days.

## Notice & Approval Process

Notify your manager as early as possible on the first day of absence, and
submit the medical certificate to HR within 3 working days of returning
to work.

## Exceptions

Absences related to a chronic condition requiring recurring treatment may
be tracked separately and are not deducted from the standard 6-week
allowance, subject to HR review of supporting documentation.

# Bereavement Leave

## Eligibility

Available to all employees, regardless of length of service, following
the death of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or
sibling).

## Duration & Pay

Employees receive 5 days of fully paid bereavement leave per qualifying
event, which may be taken non-consecutively within 30 days of the date of
passing.

## Notice & Approval Process

Inform your manager as soon as practical; no advance notice is required.
A copy of the death certificate or funeral notice may be requested by HR
for record-keeping.

## Exceptions

For the death of an extended family member (grandparent, in-law, or
similar), employees may take up to 2 days of paid leave, subject to
manager discretion.

# Family Caregiver Leave

## Eligibility

Available to employees who have completed at least 90 days of continuous
service and need to care for an immediate family member with a serious,
ongoing health condition, as certified by a registered medical
practitioner.

## Duration & Pay

Eligible employees may take up to 4 weeks of leave per calendar year,
paid at 50% of base salary. Leave may be taken continuously or in blocks
of at least one full day.

## Notice & Approval Process

Submit a request with supporting medical documentation to HR at least 2
weeks in advance where the need is foreseeable; shorter notice is
accepted for sudden medical emergencies.

## Exceptions

Leave beyond the 4-week allowance may be granted unpaid at management's
discretion, provided the employee remains in regular contact with their
manager.

# National Service & Reservist (ICT) Leave

## Eligibility

Available to all employees who are Singapore Citizens or Permanent
Residents with outstanding National Service (NS) obligations, including
In-Camp Training (ICT), NS-related medical reviews, and NS-related
examinations.

## Duration & Pay

The company grants full paid leave for the entire duration of any NS
call-up notice, in addition to the make-up pay claimable from MINDEF, so
total take-home pay is not reduced during the call-up period.

## Notice & Approval Process

Submit a copy of the official NS call-up letter to your manager and HR as
soon as it is received, so project handovers can be planned ahead of the
reporting date.

## Exceptions

Voluntary NS-related activities outside of an official call-up (e.g. an
optional refresher course) are not covered under this policy and should
be discussed with HR separately.

# Sabbatical Leave

## Eligibility

Available to employees who have completed at least 5 years of continuous
service. Limited to one sabbatical per employee every 5-year period.

## Duration & Pay

Employees may take between 4 and 12 weeks of unpaid leave. Group health
benefits continue during the sabbatical, but salary and leave accrual are
paused.

## Notice & Approval Process

Submit a written proposal to your manager at least 3 months in advance,
including a return-to-work date and a handover plan for ongoing
responsibilities.

## Exceptions

Sabbaticals for the purpose of pursuing further education directly
relevant to the employee's role may be considered for partial pay,
subject to a separate agreement with HR.

# External Client Meeting Etiquette

## Purpose & When It Applies

Applies to any meeting, call, or video conference involving an external
client or prospective client, whether initiated by the company or the
client.

## Expectations

Join at least 2 minutes early, have a clear agenda circulated beforehand,
and follow up in writing within 24 hours with any action items or
decisions made. Cameras on is encouraged but not mandatory for video
calls.

## Exceptions

For informal check-ins with long-standing clients, a lighter-touch
approach is acceptable at the account owner's discretion, though a brief
written summary is still expected.

# Internal Team Meeting Norms

## Purpose & When It Applies

Applies to recurring internal meetings such as stand-ups, sprint
planning, and team syncs.

## Expectations

Every recurring meeting should have a stated purpose and an owner
responsible for the agenda. Meetings without a clear agenda circulated in
advance may be declined by attendees. Default to 25 or 50 minute slots
rather than 30/60 to leave a buffer between back-to-back meetings.

## Exceptions

Ad hoc problem-solving huddles called to unblock urgent issues are exempt
from the agenda requirement.

# One-on-One Meeting Guidelines

## Purpose & When It Applies

Applies to regular one-on-one meetings between an employee and their
direct manager.

## Expectations

Recommended cadence is every 1-2 weeks, for 30 minutes, primarily driven
by the employee's agenda rather than status updates. Notes should be kept
privately between the manager and employee unless both agree to share
more broadly.

## Exceptions

During particularly quiet periods, either party may propose reducing
cadence to once every 3-4 weeks, by mutual agreement.

# Focus-Time & Meeting-Free Culture

## Purpose & When It Applies

Applies company-wide as a general norm for protecting uninterrupted
individual work time.

## Expectations

Teams are encouraged to keep a portion of each week meeting-free where
reasonably possible, and to default to asynchronous updates (written
status, recorded video) instead of a live meeting when a real-time
discussion isn't necessary.

## Exceptions

Client-facing meetings and time-sensitive incident response always take
priority over protecting focus time.

# Client Entertainment Expense Policy

## Eligible Expenses

Covers meals, coffee, and modest social outings (e.g. tickets to an
event) with existing or prospective clients, where the primary purpose is
relationship-building or business discussion.

## Reimbursement Limits

Reimbursable up to S$150 per person per event. Alcohol may be included
within this cap where appropriate to the setting.

## Approval & Documentation

Any single event expected to exceed S$100 per person requires manager
pre-approval. All claims require an itemised receipt and a brief note
naming the client(s) present and the business purpose.

## Exceptions

Entertainment involving immediate family members of a client is not
reimbursable, regardless of amount.

# Team Off-site & Retreat Expense Policy

## Eligible Expenses

Covers venue hire, food and beverage, and team activities for internal
team off-sites and retreats.

## Reimbursement Limits

Budgeted at up to S$75 per person per event, drawn from each department's
annual off-site budget.

## Approval & Documentation

Department heads pre-approve an annual off-site budget at the start of
the year; individual off-sites within that budget and under the
S$75/person cap do not require separate per-event approval. Itemised
receipts should still be submitted for expense tracking.

## Exceptions

Off-sites exceeding the per-person cap or the annual budget require
case-by-case approval from the department head and Finance.

# Travel & Lodging Expense Policy

## Eligible Expenses

Covers flights, ground transport, and hotel accommodation for
pre-approved business travel, whether domestic or overseas.

## Reimbursement Limits

Economy class for flights under 6 hours, premium economy permitted beyond
that. Hotel accommodation is reimbursable up to S$280 per night in most
cities, with higher caps for a small list of high-cost cities maintained
by Finance.

## Approval & Documentation

All business travel requires manager pre-approval before booking. Submit
itemised receipts for all expenses within 2 weeks of returning.

## Exceptions

Travel insurance and visa fees for business trips are separately
reimbursable and not counted against the per-night accommodation cap.

# Home Office Equipment Stipend

## Eligible Expenses

Covers a desk, chair, monitor, and other ergonomic equipment for
employees working in a hybrid or fully remote arrangement.

## Reimbursement Limits

A one-time stipend of up to S$500 is available per employee, claimable
once every 3 years.

## Approval & Documentation

Submit itemised receipts to HR within 60 days of purchase. No
pre-approval is required for purchases within the cap.

## Exceptions

Consumables (e.g. stationery) and recurring costs (e.g. home internet
bills) are not covered under this stipend.

# Professional Development Expense Policy

## Eligible Expenses

Covers course fees, certification exam fees, and conference tickets
directly relevant to an employee's current role or a clearly-defined
growth path.

## Reimbursement Limits

Up to S$1,500 per employee per calendar year, inclusive of associated
travel if the course or conference requires it.

## Approval & Documentation

Requires manager pre-approval before enrolment or registration. Submit
proof of completion (certificate or attendance confirmation) along with
the receipt to qualify for reimbursement.

## Exceptions

Degree programmes and other long-form qualifications are evaluated
separately under a case-by-case tuition-assistance arrangement, not this
policy.
